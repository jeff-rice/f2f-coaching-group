USBC Silver — Lesson Expectations

A structured, analytical lesson focused on understanding, ball motion, and long-term development.

What to Expect

A Silver-level lesson focuses on building understanding and long-term performance growth rather than quick fixes. Expect a structured, analytical, and intentional approach.

1. Initial Assessment

  • Observation before correction
  • Evaluation of timing, balance, and release
  • Ball motion analysis
  • Goal discussion and equipment review

2. Clear Diagnosis

  • Explanation of cause and effect
  • Connection between the physical game and ball reaction
  • Clear understanding of what is changing and why

3. Focused Improvement Plan

  • One primary adjustment at a time
  • Structured drill progression
  • Reinforcement through repetition and feedback

4. Lane Play & Ball Motion Discussion

  • Breakpoint and entry angle awareness
  • Pattern and transition discussion
  • Adjustment strategy (moves vs manipulation)

5. Take-Home Plan

  • 1–2 clear priorities
  • Specific drill assignment
  • Language to describe the change
  • Optional video or written notes

A Silver-level lesson should feel structured, analytical, and intentional. The goal is to build understanding, independence, and long-term performance growth.
